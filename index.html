kidney transplant facts

A kidney transplant is a surgery done to replace a diseased kidney with a healthy kidney from a donor. The kidney may come from a deceased organ donor or from a living donor.

Family members or others who are a good match may be able to donate one of their kidneys. This type of transplant is called a living transplant.

Liver transplant facts

During surgery about 40 percent to 60 percent of the donor's liver is removed. The average hospital stay for both recipient and donor is seven days;

the recovery time for donors is about two months and for recipients is about six to 12 months. The liver begins to regenerate itself almost immediately.

Lungs transplant facts

During a lung transplant, surgeons remove your diseased lung and then attach a donor lung to your airway and to the blood vessels that lead to and from your heart.

A lung transplant is a surgical procedure to replace a diseased or failing lung with a healthy lung, usually from a deceased donor.
